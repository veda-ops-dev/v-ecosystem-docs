# Project Thesis Model

## Purpose

This document defines how a project thesis is established, what it must contain,
and how it governs the planning and execution of a project inside the V Ecosystem.

It exists to answer:

```text
What makes a project worth committing to, what must be explicitly established before
a project moves toward execution, and how does the thesis constrain planning and
execution throughout the project lifecycle?
```

This is a Tier 1 ecosystem authority document.

---

## Scope

This document governs:

- what a project thesis is inside the V Ecosystem
- what a thesis must establish before a project may move toward execution
- how the thesis relates to opportunity scoring, intake outcomes, planning, and handoff
- how the thesis constrains execution scope and replanning scope
- when a thesis must be revised, superseded, or invalidated
- the anti-drift rules that prevent thesis drift, thesis inflation, and thesis-free execution

---

## Core Rule

A project must have an explicit thesis before it moves toward execution.

The thesis is the governed commitment statement that answers why this project is worth
doing, what it is expected to accomplish, and what would make it a success or a failure.

A project without an explicit thesis is not ready for handoff.
An implicit thesis — one that exists in someone's head but has not been recorded —
is not a governed thesis.

---

## Thesis Definition

A project thesis is the bounded, explicit, reviewable statement that establishes:

- why this project is being pursued
- what opportunity it is targeting
- what the expected outcome looks like if the project succeeds
- what evidence supports the belief that the opportunity is real and addressable
- what would constitute failure or grounds for revision

The thesis is not a business plan, a detailed specification, or an execution roadmap.
It is the governing narrative that makes planning and execution decisions legible in retrospect.

A thesis that cannot be stated clearly enough to be reviewed later is not a strong thesis.

---

## Relationship to Opportunity Scoring

The opportunity scoring model evaluates opportunity strength before intake.
The project thesis establishes the governing commitment statement after intake and during planning.

These are adjacent but distinct:

- scoring asks: is this opportunity strong enough to pursue?
- the thesis asks: given that we are pursuing this, what exactly are we committing to and why?

A project that scored well at intake still needs a thesis.
A strong score does not substitute for an explicit thesis.

---

## Relationship to Project Intake

A project thesis may begin forming during the intake evaluation but is not finalized
during intake. Intake produces a create-project outcome. The thesis is developed and
formalized during the planning phase that follows intake.

A thesis must be established before the project advances to handoff readiness.

---

## Required Thesis Elements

A valid project thesis must address the following elements explicitly.

### 1. Opportunity Statement
What specific opportunity is this project targeting?

The opportunity statement must be specific enough that two readers would agree on
what counts as evidence of the opportunity and what would falsify it.
Vague opportunity statements — "there is demand for content about X" — are insufficient.
The statement must be bounded: what specific gap, angle, or unmet intent is being targeted?

### 2. Evidence Basis
What evidence supports the belief that the opportunity is real?

This must reference concrete signal rather than general intuition. The evidence basis
connects the opportunity statement to VEDA observatory signal, operator-identified market
knowledge, or both. It must preserve the trust posture and freshness context of the evidence.

Weak evidence basis: "search volume exists for related terms."
Stronger evidence basis: specific VEDA signal showing intent type, demand durability,
competitive gap, or underserved angle with source and freshness context noted.

### 3. Target Outcome
What does success look like if this project performs as expected?

The target outcome must be expressed in terms the project can actually be evaluated against
post-launch. "Get organic traffic" is too vague. A more useful target outcome identifies the
traffic type, audience, or behavioral signal that would indicate the opportunity was captured.

The outcome does not need to be a precise metric commitment. It needs to be specific enough
that a later reviewer can determine whether the project is moving toward it.

### 4. Execution Scope Statement
What is the project expected to produce?

The execution scope statement defines the bounded work. It is not an exhaustive specification —
that belongs in planning and handoff docs. It is a plain-language statement of what V Forge
will be handed off to build: what kind of content, approximately what volume or structure,
and what the finished scope represents in terms of the target opportunity.

### 5. Failure and Revision Conditions
What conditions would indicate the thesis is wrong and must be revised?

Every thesis must define what would falsify it. This might include:
- signal behavior that contradicts the evidence basis
- execution that reveals the opportunity is not addressable as stated
- market change that removes the basis for the target outcome
- post-launch observation that is definitively inconsistent with the thesis

Failure and revision conditions are not weakness in the thesis. They are what make
the thesis honest and reviewable rather than unfalsifiable.

---

## Thesis Strength Standard

A thesis is strong when it is:

- **Specific** — it targets a bounded opportunity, not a broad category
- **Evidence-grounded** — it references real observable signal with trust posture
- **Actionable** — it defines a scope that planning and execution can work from
- **Falsifiable** — it defines conditions under which it would need revision
- **Concise** — it can be reviewed in a single session without requiring reconstruction

A thesis is weak when it is:

- vague about what opportunity is being targeted
- supported only by general intuition or undifferentiated search demand
- silent about failure and revision conditions
- so broad that any execution outcome would count as success
- so long that it cannot be reconstructed from the record alone

---

## Thesis Governance

### Thesis as Planning Anchor
Once established, the thesis governs planning decisions. When planning choices are made —
about content structure, depth, topic cluster, execution sequence — those choices should
be traceable to the thesis. A planning decision that cannot be explained in relation to
the thesis should be examined.

### Thesis as Handoff Constraint
The handoff to V Forge should be consistent with the thesis. An execution scope that has
drifted from the thesis without a thesis revision is a governance failure, not a natural
evolution of the project.

### Thesis as Replanning Reference
When execution findings or post-launch signal trigger reconsideration, the thesis is the
first reference. Replanning must ask: has the thesis changed, or only the execution path?
A changed thesis requires explicit thesis revision through the governed path.
A changed execution path within a still-valid thesis does not require thesis revision.

---

## Thesis Revision

A thesis must be explicitly revised when:

- the evidence basis has materially changed and the thesis no longer reflects current signal
- the target outcome is no longer achievable as stated
- the execution scope has changed in a way that changes what opportunity is being targeted
- post-launch observation reveals that the failure conditions have been met

Thesis revision is a governed planning event, not an informal update.
A revised thesis must be recorded explicitly and must reference what changed and why.
A prior thesis must not be silently replaced without a governed revision.

The invalidation and supersedence doctrine applies to thesis revision:
`../governance/invalidation-and-supersedence-doctrine.md`

---

## Thesis Inflation Failure Mode

Thesis inflation is the pattern where the thesis expands during planning or execution
to justify an expanding scope, without the expansion being explicitly grounded in
stronger evidence or changed conditions.

Signs of thesis inflation:
- the opportunity statement grows vague to accommodate more content directions
- the target outcome grows larger to match execution ambition rather than signal
- the evidence basis weakens as scope widens
- the failure conditions are silently removed because the thesis has grown too broad to falsify

Thesis inflation must not be treated as natural project growth.
It is a governance failure that makes replanning, evaluation, and honest assessment impossible.

---

## Anti-Drift Rules

The thesis has drifted when:

- the project is executing on content that cannot be connected to the opportunity statement
- the evidence basis has been forgotten or replaced by current intuition
- the target outcome has been restated to match whatever the project produced
- no failure condition is tracked because the thesis was never falsifiable
- the thesis exists only as a memory, not as a recoverable governing record

These are not signs of a maturing project.
They are signs that the project is no longer governed.

---

## LLM Use Principle

A capable LLM should be able to infer from this doc that:

- a thesis is a required governed artifact, not a summary written after the fact
- scoring an opportunity and establishing a thesis are distinct activities
- a thesis that cannot be falsified is not a governed thesis
- thesis revision is a governed event, not a casual update
- planning and execution decisions should be traceable to the thesis

If a project is in execution but has no recoverable thesis, the planning governance
has failed regardless of how well execution is proceeding.

## Usage

This document should be used:

- when moving a project from intake-outcome to planning to confirm a thesis is required
- when reviewing whether a project's planning choices are traceable to a thesis
- when deciding whether a planning or scope change requires thesis revision
- when preparing for handoff to confirm the thesis is still governing the scope
- when post-launch findings require reconsideration and the thesis must be the first reference
